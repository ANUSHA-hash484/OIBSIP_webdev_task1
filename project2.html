What is Yoga?

Yoga is a group of physical, mental, and spiritual practices that originated in ancient India. It aims to control and still the mind, bringing harmony between the body and spirit through postures, breathing techniques, and meditation.

10 Popular Styles of Yoga

Hatha Yoga

Gentle foundational style—great for beginners. physical postures (asanas) and breathing techniques (pranayama) to achieve physical and mental balance and prepare the body for deeper spiritual practices. It's often described as the "yoga of force" or the "yoga of balance," aiming to harmonize the body, mind, and spirit.

Vinyasa Yoga

Dynamic, flowing sequences synchronized with breath. Vinyasa yoga is a dynamic style of yoga characterized by flowing sequences of poses linked with breath, often called "flow yoga." It emphasizes movement and breath coordination, leading to a more active and invigorating practice than some other yoga styles

Ashtanga Yoga

Structured, vigorous series building discipline. Ashtanga yoga is a dynamic style of yoga characterized by a specific sequence of postures (asanas) linked with breath (pranayama) and gaze (drishti). It's often described as a moving meditation due to its focus on synchronizing breath and movement. The practice is structured around eight limbs

Iyengar Yoga

Alignment-focused practice using props for precision. "Iyengar Yoga" combined with "big size" typically refers to specialized equipment designed to support practitioners of various sizes in Iyengar yoga, such as large yoga chairs or oversized bolsters and blocks. These larger-sized props are often designed with greater weight capacity or dimensions to accommodate a wider range of body types and provide adequate support during yoga practice.

Kundalini Yoga

Spiritual practice with breathwork, chanting, meditation. Kundalini yoga is a form of yoga focused on awakening a spiritual energy, often depicted as a coiled serpent (kundalini) at the base of the spine, through practices like breathwork, meditation, and chanting

Yin Yoga

Slow, meditative style with long-held poses for flexibility. Yin yoga is a slow-paced, meditative style of yoga that focuses on holding poses for extended periods, typically 3-5 minutes, to target deep connective tissues like fascia, ligaments, and joints. It contrasts with more active "yang" styles of yoga by emphasizing stillness and passive stretching

Restorative Yoga

Deep relaxation with supported poses to soothe the nervous system Restorative yoga is a gentle, passive style of yoga that emphasizes relaxation and stress reduction through supported, long-held poses

Hot / Bikram Yoga

Fixed, heated-room practice (~40 °C) for flexibility and detox It was developed by Bikram Choudhury and is known for its intense, sweat-inducing workouts aimed at improving flexibility, strength, and overall well-being

Power Yoga

Fitness-focused, high-intensity Vinyasa-style practice for strength Power yoga is a dynamic and physically demanding style of yoga that originated in the United States in the 1990s, evolving from Ashtanga and Bikram yoga

Bhakti Yoga

Devotional practice emphasizing chanting, prayer, connection Bhakti Yoga, also known as the path of devotion, is a spiritual practice within Hinduism focused on loving devotion to a personal deity. It's one of the three classical paths to liberation (moksha) in Hinduism, alongside Jnana (knowledge) and Karma (action) yoga

International Day of Yoga

Celebrated on 21 June. The 2025 theme is “Yoga for One Earth, One Health”, with global participation in mass events. Recognizing its universal appeal, on 11 December 2014, the United Nations proclaimed 21 June as the International Day of Yoga by resolution 69/131. The International Day of Yoga aims to raise awareness worldwide of the many benefits of practicing yoga.

Benefits of Yoga

  • Improves Flexibility: Regular yoga practice stretches and tones the body muscles.
  • Boosts Strength: Many yoga poses build core, leg, and arm strength.
  • Reduces Stress: Yoga calms the mind through deep breathing and meditation.
  • Improves Posture: Yoga aligns the spine and strengthens postural muscles.
  • Enhances Concentration: Focused breathing and poses improve mental clarity.
